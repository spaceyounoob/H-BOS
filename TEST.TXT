Just exactly like father and the other men of that time and country who were no more than walking shells for this compact and dangerous mass of the family ghosts which had gone before it and had been the foundation and the beginning of the land itself and all its people and history and tradition, even including the women who were no better than the men and perhaps even worse since they had to spend so much more time being women and so could devote so much more concentrated and vicious attention to being the custodians of the family's private legend as it was actually preserved in the old square rooms and the long cool high-ceilinged halls where the quiet light of the quiet lamps lay like pale gold upon the stiff heavy linen of the table and the dim faded chintz of the sofas and the heavy silver of the coffee service which didn’t belong to them but to the very ghosts whose custodians they were, acting as though this were the first time the old story had been told or the old threat had been made or the old tragedy had been lived through again, as though they were living through the long dull quiet uneventful years which led up to the war and then the war itself and the years of slow decline and the dying off of the men and the old women finally taking refuge behind their black lace caps and the shuttered windows of big empty houses where the eye could not see from any point.
